Recurring Deposit (RD)
Monthly Saving

A Recurring Deposit lets you invest a fixed amount every month and earn compounding interest. It's perfect for salaried individuals who want to build wealth gradually without locking in a large sum upfront.

How it works: Each monthly instalment earns interest from the date of deposit until maturity.

SML Group RD offers 10–12% p.a. depending on tenure (1 yr: 10%, 2 yrs: 11%, 3–4 yrs: 12%), compounded monthly — significantly higher than bank RDs (SBI: 6.5–7.0%, HDFC: 7.0–7.5%).

For example, depositing ₹5,000/month for 3 years at 12% p.a. yields a maturity value of approx ₹2,15,000 — vs ₹1,98,000 from a bank RD at 7%.

Non-Convertible Debentures (NCD)
Debt Instrument

A Non-Convertible Debenture (NCD) is a debt security issued by a company to raise funds from the public. Unlike convertible debentures, NCDs cannot be converted into equity shares — you receive fixed interest and your principal back at maturity.

Why NCDs? They are governed by SEBI/RBI regulations, backed by the company's assets, and offer higher returns than bank FDs. They are particularly suitable for investors seeking long-term, predictable income.

SML Finance NCD: 10-year tenure · 12.5–13% monthly payout · Min ₹2 Lakhs. Premature closure allowed after 1 year with reducing penalty (nil after 3 years).

Subordinated Deposit (SD)
Fixed Deposit

A Subordinated Deposit is a fixed-term instrument offered by RBI-registered NBFCs. You deposit a lump sum and earn fixed interest paid monthly or annually over 5.5 years.

It is called "subordinated" because, in a hypothetical liquidation scenario, these depositors are repaid after secured creditors — in exchange for which they earn a significantly higher interest rate than bank FDs.

No premature closure is permitted. At maturity, your full principal plus accumulated interest (annual payout) is returned. The monthly payout option provides a steady income stream throughout the tenure.
